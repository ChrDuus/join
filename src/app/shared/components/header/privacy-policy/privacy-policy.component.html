Legal Basis

In the following privacy policy, we provide transparent information about the legal principles and regulations, i.e., the legal bases of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), which enable us to process personal data.
As far as the GDPR is concerned, we only process your data where at least one of the following applies:

  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR): You have given us consent to process data for a specific purpose. An example would be storing your entered data in a contact form.
  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b) GDPR): To fulfill a contract or pre-contractual obligations with you, we process your data. For example, if we conclude a purchase contract with you, we need personal information in advance.
  • Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c) GDPR): If we are subject to a legal obligation, we process your data. For example, we are legally required to keep invoices for accounting purposes. These usually contain personal data.
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR): We process data based on legitimate interests unless your fundamental rights and freedoms prevail. For example, we must process certain data to operate our website securely and efficiently. This processing is thus a legitimate interest.

As a rule, additional legal bases apply alongside the GDPR. For example, national laws such as the Austrian Data Protection Act (DSG) or the German Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG). If other legal bases are relevant, we will indicate them in the following sections.

If you have questions about the legal basis, feel free to contact us via the contact details listed in the legal notice or this privacy policy.

Introduction to Web Hosting

What is web hosting?
When you visit websites today, certain information – including personal data – is automatically generated and stored, including on this website. This data should be processed as sparingly as possible and only with justification. By website, we mean the totality of all web pages on a domain, i.e., everything from the homepage to the last subpage (like example.com/start or example.com/contact).

When you want to view a website on a screen, you use a program called a web browser. You enter a web address and the browser sends a request to another computer, the so-called web server. This web server then sends the required data back to your browser, which displays it on your screen. In this process, certain data is transmitted and stored either temporarily or permanently on the web server. This includes, for example:

  • the IP address of your device (e.g., 192.168.0.1)
  • browser and browser version (e.g., Chrome 87)
  • the operating system used (e.g., Windows 10)
  • the address (URL) of the accessed web page
  • the date and time
  • so-called HTTP status codes (e.g., “404 Not Found”)

As a rule, this data is stored for two weeks and then automatically deleted. We do not disclose this data, but we cannot rule out that it may be viewed by authorities in the event of unlawful behavior.

In short: Your visit to our site generates data, and this data may be stored for a short time. We only use it where necessary and in accordance with the law.
